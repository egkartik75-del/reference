What is IQAC?

The Internal Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC) is the apex body for planning, guiding and monitoring all quality-related activities of the institution as per NAAC guidelines. It ensures continuous improvement in teaching, learning, research, and administration.

Objectives & Functions

Academic & Administrative Audits
Systematic review of teaching-learning, examinations, infrastructure, and administrative processes.
AQAR Preparation & Submission
Annual Quality Assurance Reports compiled and submitted to NAAC as per prescribed timelines.
Stakeholder Feedback System
Regular collection and analysis of feedback from students, faculty, alumni, parents, and employers.
Faculty Development Programmes
Organizing FDPs, workshops, seminars for continuous faculty skill enhancement and NEP alignment.
Outcome Based Education (OBE)
Ensuring implementation of OBE framework and aligning curriculum with graduate attributes.
Green & Gender Audits
Annual green campus audit and gender sensitization audit as per NAAC Criteria 7 requirements.

IQAC Composition 2025-26

Constituted as per UGC/NAAC guidelines. Tenure: July 2025 – June 2026.

Best Practices & Institutional Distinctiveness

As per NAAC Criterion 6.5 – documented best practices with objectives, context, and outcomes.

BEST PRACTICE 01
Peer-Assisted Learning Programme

Senior students mentor juniors in a structured programme that improves academic outcomes and builds leadership among students.

Outcome: 18% improvement in pass percentage in mentored batches (2024-25)
BEST PRACTICE 02
Village Outreach & Digital Literacy Drive

NSS volunteers conduct digital literacy workshops in Seema valley villages, aligning education with community development.

Outcome: 300+ community members trained in basic digital skills (2024-25)
INSTITUTIONAL DISTINCTIVENESS
Remote Hill Region Access to Higher Education

Located in a remote Himalayan valley, the college provides quality higher education to tribal and rural youth of Seema-Rohru belt, many of whom are first-generation learners.

Impact: Consistent increase in enrollment from remote panchayats over last 5 years
INSTITUTIONAL DISTINCTIVENESS
Eco-Sensitive Campus Conservation

Campus maintains a natural heritage tree inventory and runs an annual biodiversity documentation drive with science students.

Impact: 120+ tree species documented; campus declared plastic-free zone 2023
